$1,000 a month from digital products sounds like a goal. It is actually a math problem.

You need to sell a certain number of things at a certain price. That is the whole equation. The question is just: which things, at what price, to which people, how often.

Most creators never get there because they spend their time on the wrong part of that equation.

## The Mistake That Keeps People Stuck at Zero

A lot of creators spend energy on visibility (more content, more platforms, more posting) before they have a product that consistently converts.

If you are getting traffic but not making sales, more traffic is not the fix. You have a conversion problem, not an awareness problem. More people seeing a page that does not sell anything is just more people leaving.

The first step to $1K a month is a product that actually sells. Everything else gets built on top of that.

## What Consistent Monthly Income Actually Requires

There are three things that have to be in place before income becomes consistent.

**A product people pay for.** This sounds obvious. But a lot of creators skip validating this and jump straight to growing an audience. A product that has been purchased even once by a stranger is worth more than an idea that has not.

**A clear path from discovery to purchase.** Someone finds your content, or lands on your page. What happens next? Is it obvious where to go? Is the product easy to understand and buy? If that path has friction, consistency will not come from posting more.

**A repeatable way to bring in new people.** This does not have to be complicated. It could be one type of content, one SEO article, one community you show up in. But there needs to be something that keeps the top of the path filled.

Those three things together are what generate consistent income. Optimizing any one of them without the others in place will not get you to $1K a month reliably.

### The Number That Helps

$1,000 a month at $9 per product is about 112 sales per month, roughly four per day.

At $19 per product it is about 53 sales per month.

At a mix of prices with a bundle or two, it is less than you probably think.

Running the actual math makes the goal feel more real and less abstract. It also makes clear which lever matters most for your specific setup.

## Two Things to Focus On First

**1. Identify your one product that is easiest to sell.**

Not the one you are most proud of. The one where the value is clearest, the audience is most specific, and the price feels like an obvious trade. Build your first repeatable system around that product.

**2. Find one channel that brings in buyers, not just viewers.**

Not followers. Buyers. Where do the people who have already purchased come from? Do more of that, not something new.
